Gaurang Bansal is a distinguished scholar currently in final year of Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from [National University of Singapore (NUS)](https://nus.edu.sg/). His exceptional expertise in the domains of Privacy and Security earned him the prestigious [Google PhD Fellowship](https://research.google/outreach/phd-fellowship/recipients/) award. Additionally, he has been honored with the [NUS President Fellowship](https://cde.nus.edu.sg/ece/graduate/scholarship-financial-support/) Award in recognition of his outstanding academic achievements. Previously, he had completed his Master's and Bachelor's from [BITS Pilani](https://www.bits-pilani.ac.in/) in 2020 & 2018, respectively.

Gaurang's research interests span a wide spectrum, including cryptography, security, deep learning, LLMs. His research portfolio consists of 30+ publications, featured in top-tier conferences and journals such as IEEE S&P, IEEE TVT, IEEE INFOCOM, and others. His research has garnered significant recognition, evident through more than 700 citations on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=A-t6Ds8AAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao).

With a wealth of professional experience, Gaurang Bansal has demonstrated his expertise in various roles. He currently serves as a Research Collaborator with [Google](https://about.google/intl/ALL_in/) since July 2023, while concurrently working as Senior Researcher at Tinker Labs, BITS Pilani, which he assumed in August 2017. Notably, in addition to these roles, he has also contributed to the success of [DRIFE](https://www.drife.io/)as a Researcher Lead engineering a decentralized ride-sharing platform akin to Uber, enabling dApps deployment for advanced mobility solutions, fostering a peer-to-peer transportation network. His ability to excel in multiple positions simultaneously showcases his exceptional skills and dedication in the field.

He has enthusiastically taught a broad range of topics, imparting his expertise to students from institutions like the University of Pittsburgh, NUS, BITS Pilani, and Microsoft, among others. During his time at NUS and BITS, his teaching portfolio included courses in Computer Networks, Artificial Intelligence, and Machine Learning.
